Why web dev? – FAQs

Why is your current path not right for you? +

Some people I know found their career path instantly. Bang! I want to be a rocket scientist! And they go on to be one for the rest of their life, loving it in the process. For most, it is a series of diagonal movements that we gather clues about our next destination.

Project management is enjoyable, but for me, I enjoy feeling overwhelmed by information and problems — perhaps this is due to growing up with a Dad who is an engineer.

Why is web development the right path for you? +

When I first started, I thought there was no chance I could do it, and it was far-fetched for me even to consider it as a path. However, after working my way through The Odin Project, I realised that it is essential to overcome these doubts to learn this craft. Everyone has them when they start.

Coding challenges you intellectually straight away and continues to. To get better, it is vital to keep writing code, failing and ensure you are kind to yourself. I love this.

I also love the detail-orientated nature of the craft. Shall I use a switch case or event listener? How should I name my classes? There is no definitive answer to these questions, but as you continue learning, you form educated opinions that build the foundation of your knowledge.

The more time I code, the more I love it. That is why it is the right path for me.

Why FAC, in particular? +

The Odin Project was brilliant. It was free, an excellent curriculum, and it threw you in the deep end to work things out on your own. As I near the completion of the foundation course, I want to extend my knowledge, build my ability to talk about it, teach it and create code collaboratively with others.

It is a gratifying activity to do on your own, but you need the ability to communicate the reasons behind the code to be successful in the industry. The cherry on top is the non-profit nature of FAC. Supporting a non-profit initiative through participation falls firmly within my morals.

What are your coding dreams? +

I want to feel confident in each piece of code that I write and not have that imposter syndrome lingering.

It’s probably too ambitious of a dream!

Who is a coding inspiration to you? +

I work with a developer, Marc, at my current job. His attention to detail and ability to hone down to the ‘why’ of each line of code are phenomenal, as well as his high skill in asking the right questions. I suspect this is why he rarely deploys buggy code.
